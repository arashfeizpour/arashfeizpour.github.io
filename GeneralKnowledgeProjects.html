1 - Second Order Differential Equation Constants of Integration (two variables, two equations):
Having a second order differential equation, you have to find the constants of integration through boundary conditions. As you know, a n-order differential equation needs n boundary conditions so we can find the constants through solving n variables, n equations.
In this program we want to solve a two variable two equations to find the constants of integration of a second order differential equation. Setting the boundary conditions to be: ti1 = 0.0s and xi1 = 10.0m, ti2 = 1.0s and xi2 = 8.0m and constants: k = 20 and m = 2kg, we will get:
OUTPUTs :
[ 0.3048407017491] [-16.0742579474315]

Point: If you needed to solve n variables, n equations, you just have to add more boundary conditions (n total) and use them to make more equations (n total) and assign your own main equation to equation variable.
