1 - Solving a Second Order Eq. using Euler-Richardson Method:
We found the velocities of a falling styrophoam using forward and central difference method in the “Using Central and Forward Difference Approximation to Find Velocity Values” article in one of the projects here. Here we want to go the other way around and find velocities using acceleration and time instead of positions and time.
In this program we want to calculate the velocity of the water (with respect to time) which is being pushed out of a cylindrical container by a dense piston.

2 - Solving a Simple ODE using First-Order Runge-Kutta:
Do you want to know how to work out a ODE using Runge-Kutta (first order)? you can simply make the loop a little longer and change it to a higher order . .
In this program we want to solve a pretty simple ordinary differential equation numerically using only the first order Runge-Kutta. This numerical method is one of the most strong ones yet and has a high accuracy. If you get the handle of it (and you will by looking at the codes), you can use it in higher orders easily for you own scientific problems.
The equation we want to solve is : du/dx = -2*u + x + 4

It is easy – yet not implemented here – to plot the analytical result and check out the error function to see the power of Runge-Kutta (you can plot Runge-Kutta results for different orders and see how they change as you go to a higher order as well)

3 - Solving a Damped Harmonic Motion Equation (Runge Kutta):
All of the real springs are non-ideal and have a damped motion, meaning the amplitude of oscillations of their energy, acceleration, position, velocity reduces with time till they are ocillating no more.

In this program we want to illustrate the behaviour of a damepd spring-mass system (hanging) through solving dv / dt = ‘- (2 * gamma * v + w0 ** 2 * x)’ (where gamma is the damping constant and w0 is the angular frequency) using Runge-Kutta method and plotting its diagrams. You can easily change the gamma variable through changing c and see how the damping constant aletrs the diagram.

4 - Comparing Numerical Methods Through Conservation of Energy:
Knowing the fact that the best numerical method we should find is rather case-dependent, we want to compare Euler, Euler-Cromer, Euler-Richardson, and Verlet in the case of solving a second order differential equation to find the energies of a simple harmonic oscillator and check how conservation of energy stands for these numerical methods.
In this program we want to solve a pretty simple second order differential equation numerically using the aforementioned methods for a simple harmonic oscillator. Then, we want to find the energy of this motion as we know that energy is conserved and is always equal to the potential energy of the system at its turning points. Looking at the codes you can see that every method has a function for solving the equation to find the position and velocity of spring-mass motion and another fucntion to find the energy difference at different times for each method through the conservation of energy.
The equations we want to solve are : dx/dt = v , dv/dt = -w0**2 * x , dt/dt = 1
